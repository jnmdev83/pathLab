Understanding the Tiers of Screening

detection_and_zone

Tumor Markers

Blood tests that look for proteins or substances made by cancer cells. Essential for initial screening.

genetics

Genetic Predisposition

Advanced screening that analyzes specific genes linked to hereditary cancer risks like BRCA1/BRCA2.

monitor_heart

Systemic Impact

Comprehensive profiles that include organ function tests to see how the whole body is performing.

Frequently Asked Questions

What's typically included in a cancer screening package? expand_more
Our packages typically include a combination of Tumor Markers (like PSA, CEA, CA 125), Complete Blood Count (CBC) with differential, Metabolic panels, and specific organ function tests (Liver, Kidney). Higher-tier packages may include radiological screening guidance.
Who should consider these packages? expand_more
They are recommended for adults over 35, individuals with a family history of cancer, long-term smokers, or those exposed to environmental toxins. It is a proactive step for anyone prioritizing longevity.
How often should I get screened? expand_more
For preventive health, an annual screening is generally recommended. However, frequency depends on your individual risk factors and age. Consult with a physician for a personalized schedule.
